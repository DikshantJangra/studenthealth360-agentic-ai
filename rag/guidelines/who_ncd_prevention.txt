WHO — Global Action Plan for the Prevention and Control of Noncommunicable Diseases (NCDs)
Source: World Health Organization (2013–2030)

OVERVIEW
Noncommunicable diseases — including cardiovascular disease, diabetes, cancer, and chronic respiratory disease — are the leading cause of death globally, accounting for 74% of all deaths. Many NCDs are preventable through addressing shared risk factors: tobacco use, unhealthy diet, physical inactivity, and harmful use of alcohol.

DIETARY RECOMMENDATIONS
The WHO recommends a balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins. Specifically:
- Limit free sugar intake to less than 10% of total energy intake (ideally below 5%).
- Reduce sodium intake to less than 5 grams per day to prevent hypertension.
- Consume at least 400 grams of fruits and vegetables per day.
- Replace saturated fats with unsaturated fats; limit trans-fat intake.
- Increase dietary fibre intake through whole grains, legumes, and vegetables.

CARDIOVASCULAR RISK REDUCTION
Elevated blood pressure (hypertension) is the single largest risk factor for cardiovascular disease. Key interventions include:
- Regular blood pressure monitoring; target below 140/90 mmHg for the general population.
- Reduce dietary sodium and increase potassium-rich foods.
- Engage in at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic physical activity per week.
- Maintain a healthy body weight (BMI 18.5–24.9).
- Avoid tobacco and limit alcohol consumption.

PHYSICAL ACTIVITY GUIDELINES
Adults aged 18–64 should undertake:
- At least 150–300 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic physical activity per week.
- Or at least 75–150 minutes of vigorous-intensity aerobic physical activity per week.
- Muscle-strengthening activities involving all major muscle groups on 2 or more days per week.
- Limit sedentary time; any amount of physical activity is better than none.

MENTAL HEALTH AND WELL-BEING
Mental health is integral to overall health. The WHO emphasises:
- Promotion of mental well-being through social connectedness and resilience-building.
- Early identification and intervention for stress, anxiety, and depressive disorders.
- Access to mental health services in educational and workplace settings.
- Reducing stigma associated with seeking mental health support.

MONITORING AND SURVEILLANCE
Regular health screenings are recommended, including:
- Annual blood pressure and cholesterol checks for individuals over 18.
- Fasting blood glucose screening every 3 years for at-risk populations.
- BMI and waist circumference measurement during routine health visits.
